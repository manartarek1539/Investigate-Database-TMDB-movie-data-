Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue.¶

  • Certain columns, like cast and genres, contain multiple values separated by pipe (|) characters.
  • There are some odd characters in the cast column.
  • The final two columns ending with _adj show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  • Which year has the highest release of movies?
  • What is the most common average vote?
  • What is correlation between "revenue, budget and movie popularity, ..."?
  • Who is the top 10 Directors?
  • Which Genre Has The Highest Release Of Movies?
  • Who is the actor that has The Highest Release Of Movies?

Which Genre Has The Highest Release Of Movies?¶

In [21]:
#create function to split genres & cast and return the counts of each genres and actor

def split_data(x):
    #concatenate the content of all rows with seperator |
    concat_data = df[x].str.cat(sep='|')
    #split the above data and store it in pandas series
    splitted_data =pd.Series(concat_data.split('|'))
    #counts values for each genres or actor
    counts = splitted_data.value_counts()
    
    return counts

Conclusions¶

  • Our dataset contains data of movies from 1960 : 2015, The highest budget was 425000000 dollars and The highest revenue was 2781505847 dollars .
  • Year (2014) has the highest number of released movies (700 movies).
  • Average vote (6.1) is the most common Average Vote given to movies (455 times).
  • There is positive correlation between (Budget and Revenue),(Popularity and Renvenue) and (Vote Average and Popularity) and (Vote Average and Revenue).
  • The director "Woody Allen" has the highest number of released movies and comes next "Clint Eastwood".
  • The highest number of released movies were "Drama" genres and then comes "Comedy" genres.
  • The actor "Robert Di Niro" has the highest number of released movies and then comes "Samuel L.Jackson".
